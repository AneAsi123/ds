Dark Souls III (2016) is the epic conclusion to the trilogy. The world is finally reaching its absolute end—the First Flame is about to die forever, and the land itself is literally collapsing and folding in on itself. You play as the "Ashen One," an undead warrior who fails to link the fire in the past but is woken up from the grave to hunt down the Lords of Cinder (powerful rulers who abandoned their thrones) and force them to save the world one last time.

Key Features:

  • Faster and Smoother Combat: Dark Souls III combined the heavy, tactical combat of the first game with the fast, aggressive speed of Bloodborne (another popular game by FromSoftware). Combat feels incredibly smooth, active, and satisfying.

  • Weapon Arts (Skills): This game introduced "Weapon Arts"—unique special moves and abilities for every weapon in the game. Whether it is a stance that breaks an enemy's shield or a magical spin attack, it added a whole new layer of strategy to battles.

  • Incredible Visuals and Music: Being built for newer consoles, the game features breathtaking views of giant gothic castles, ruined cities, and beautiful sunsets. The orchestral soundtrack is also widely considered the best and most dramatic in the series.

  • Incredible bosses: Dark Souls III is universally praised for having some of the absolute best boss fights in video game history. The battles feel like tragic, epic dances accompanied by incredible orchestral music. Each encounter is a spectacular test of skill.
