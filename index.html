Introduction

The Dark Souls action RPG trilogy, created by the Japanese studio FromSoftware, is one of the most influential video game series in history. It is famous for its high difficulty, amazing world design, and a deep, hidden story.

Introduction to the Story

"In the Age of Ancients the world was unformed, shrouded by fog. A land of gray crags, Archtrees and Everlasting Dragons."

The world of Dark Souls changed forever when the First Flame appeared deep underground. Inside the fire, powerful beings found the "Lord Souls." Using this power, they defeated the ancient dragons and started the Age of Fire—an era of light, prosperity, and life.

But no fire burns forever. When the First Flame started to fade, darkness began to take over, and a terrible curse fell upon humanity: the Darksign. Cursed humans cannot die. Instead, they become Undead. Every time they die and wake up again, they lose a part of their mind until they become mindless monsters called Hollows.

In each game you play as the chosen one who has to sacrifice themself to link the First Flame or you can let it fade and start the Age of Dark. Fans often debate about what age is better. If the player choses to prolong the Age of Fire the fire bursts back to life, the curse of the Undead is temporarily broken, and light returns to the world, but This is only a temporary fix. You sacrifice yourself to save a dying world, but eventually, the fire will start to fade again. The cycle will repeat, and another Undead will have to make the same sacrifice in the future. If the player decides to let the Age of Dark start the Age of Fire ends, and the world is covered in darkness. Power shifts away from the old gods and goes to humanity, who are naturally born from the Dark. While it sounds like freedom for humans, the "Age of Dark" is terrifying and unknown. Without light, the world becomes a cold, shadowy place filled with abyss-born monsters, leaving the future completely uncertain.
