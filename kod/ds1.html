Dark Souls (2011) is the masterpiece that started a whole new sub-genre of gaming (often called "Soulslike"). The game plunges you into the interconnected, dying kingdom of Lordran. As the "Chosen Undead," your journey begins in a gloomy asylum and takes you through the atmospheric Firelink Shrine all the way to Anor Londo, the breathtaking city of the Gods.

Key Features of the Game:

  • Brilliant World Design: Lordran is famous for its vertical and clever map layout. The entire world is connected like a giant puzzle. Opening a secret shortcut after hours of struggling, only to realize you are back at a familiar bonfire, is an amazing feeling.

  • Iconic and Fair Difficulty: While the game is famous for being incredibly hard, it is usually fair. Every death is a lesson. By learning enemy attack patterns and managing your stamina, you slowly transform from a weak undead into a powerful warrior.

  • Memorable Boss Fights: The battles in this game are legendary. Confronting massive creatures like the Great Grey Wolf Sif, or surviving the brutal duo of Dragon Slayer Ornstein and Executioner Smough, provides an unmatched feeling of triumph when you finally win.

  • Unique Multiplayer System: Players can leave helpful (or intentionally misleading) glowing messages on the ground for others to read. You can also summon other players to help you beat a difficult boss, or invade another player's world as a phantom for player-versus-player (PvP) combat.
